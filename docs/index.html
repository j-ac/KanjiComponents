Kanji - The Way They Were Meant To Be

Kanji are so hard because they were never meant for Japanese.

They were made first for Chinese, and Japanese scholars used them for convenience. 1500 years have passed since then and it understandably has made the problem worse. But Kanji was at one time an elegant and logical system.

If you know how to look, sometimes the original logic behind the characters is still there.

Yet no popular kanji approach I know of leverages this. Most methods both rely heavily on mnemonics, even for kanji that survive until the present day with the original message intact.

This site exists to catalogue every character that becomes clearer once you understand its real structure.
